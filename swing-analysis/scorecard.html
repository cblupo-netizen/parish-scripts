Coaching reference

The cue to say and a drill or two for each killer. Give one thing at a time.

1. Lunging / drifting · Face-on

"Stay back — let it come to you. Turn, don't lunge."

Drills: fence/wall stride-and-hold · tee work with weight held back · one-handed top-hand tee.

2. Casting / bat drag · Behind

"Knob to the ball. Keep your hands inside."

Drills: towel/connection-ball pinned under lead armpit · inside-pitch tee · two-tee drill (don't clip the outer tee).

3. Pulling off / flying open · Face-on

"Stay closed, see it deep, hit it back up the middle."

Drills: opposite-field tee/soft-toss · contact point set deeper · "show the logo" — chest closed until the hands go.

4. No load / no separation · Face-on

"Get a rhythm, gather, then go. Hips lead, hands follow."

Drills: toe-tap / small leg-kick timing · "hands back as foot goes down" reps · hip-turn-first dry swings.

5. Stepping in the bucket · Behind / Face-on

"Stride to the pitcher, not away from it."

Drills: stride into a bat laid on the ground as a guide · closed-toe stride vs. a wall · slow walk-up swings toward the mound.

6. Steep / long bat path · Behind

"Match the plane. Stay through the zone, not down or up at it."

Drills: two-tee path drill (low back tee, higher front tee) · high-tee/low-tee mix · bottom-hand-only path reps.

How to use it: score each killer OK / Minor / Major → pick the top 1–3 Majors only → tell the hitter what you saw + the cue + one drill → refilm in 2–3 weeks and re-score. Did it move? That's your proof. Honest caveat: these are rough, widely-taught fundamentals, not gospel — trust your eyes and catch the big, obvious stuff.
