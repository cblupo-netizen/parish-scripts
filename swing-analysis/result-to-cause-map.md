# Working Backward: Result → Cause → Fix

The honest way to diagnose a rec swing. Start from **what the ball actually did**
(or what you felt) — that narrows the possible causes to two or three before you
even watch the video. Then the video just confirms *which* of those causes is
yours. You never grade a swing against an imaginary ideal; you explain why the
result the hitter already saw is happening.

Pair this with `swing-scorecard.md` — the six swing-killers there are the **cause
library** this map points into.

> How to read it: find the row that matches the result. Check the **likely
> causes** in order (most common first). Open the video at the listed angle to
> confirm. Give the hitter the fix + one drill. Most results have more than one
> possible cause — the video breaks the tie.

---

## The map

### Result: Late on the fastball — swinging through it, fouling it off late, or weak contact deep in the zone
- **Likely causes (in order):**
  1. **Front foot gets down late** — the heel isn't planted before the ball is on you, so there's no time to fire. *(timing, not mechanics — a clean swing can still be late)*
  2. Load/gather starts too late (not synced to the pitcher's release).
  3. Long swing — casting (see scorecard #2) adds time the fastball doesn't give you.
- **Confirm on video (face-on):** Count frames from pitcher's release to front-heel plant. Foot should be **down at or before release**. If it plants after, that's it.
- **Fix:** "Foot down early — be loaded *before* you decide to swing."
- **Drill:** Foot-down-on-release reps off a pitcher's windup; metronome/rhythm timing; start the gather earlier.
- ⚠️ This is the one good coaches most often miss, because the swing *shape* looks fine. Timing is its own axis.

### Result: Rollover grounder to the pull side (weak chopper to SS/3B for a righty)
- **Likely causes:**
  1. **Pulling off / front shoulder flying open** (scorecard #3) — body opens early, barrel rolls over the top of the ball.
  2. **Casting** (scorecard #2) — hands get around the ball instead of through it.
  3. Swinging at a pitch away but trying to pull it.
- **Confirm on video (face-on + behind):** Is the front shoulder open before contact? Do the hands cast out instead of leading with the knob?
- **Fix:** "Stay closed, hit it back up the middle / oppo."
- **Drill:** Opposite-field tee and soft-toss; "knob to the ball"; two-tee inside drill.

### Result: Pop-ups and lazy fly balls — getting *under* everything
- **Likely causes:**
  1. Back shoulder drops / barrel works too far uphill (steep up, scorecard #6).
  2. Lunging/drifting (scorecard #1) drops the back side.
- **Confirm on video (behind):** Does the barrel dive under the ball and loop up, finishing way high?
- **Fix:** "Match the plane — stay through the ball, not under it."
- **Drill:** Two-tee path drill (low-back / higher-front); high-tee reps; bottom-hand path work.

### Result: Topping the ball / chopping weak grounders
- **Likely causes:**
  1. Bat path too steep *down* (scorecard #6).
  2. Casting (scorecard #2) — barrel never gets behind the ball on plane.
  3. Lunging out front so contact happens past the barrel's good window (scorecard #1).
- **Confirm on video (behind):** Steep downward chop across the plane? Hands casting out early?
- **Fix:** "Get on plane behind the ball and stay through the zone."
- **Drill:** Two-tee path drill; tee at contact-point depth; connection-ball reps.

### Result: Way early / out front on off-speed, rolling over or lunging
- **Likely causes:**
  1. Drifting/lunging forward (scorecard #1) — committing the weight too early.
  2. No real load to hold back against (scorecard #4).
- **Confirm on video (face-on):** Head and weight travel toward the pitcher before the swing fires.
- **Fix:** "Stay back, let it travel — turn, don't lunge."
- **Drill:** Stride-and-hold against a fence; hit off a held-back weight; off-speed-only soft toss.

### Result: Can only go oppo, can't pull anything, everything's weak the other way
- **Likely causes:**
  1. Consistently very late (see "late on the fastball" — same timing cause).
  2. No aggression / no load to drive through the ball (scorecard #4).
- **Confirm on video (face-on):** Late foot-down + passive, armsy swing with no gather.
- **Fix:** "Start earlier, get a load, be on time — then let the swing go."
- **Drill:** Earlier gather/timing reps; rhythm with a toe-tap or small leg-kick.

### Result: Stepping out, bailing, can't cover the outside pitch
- **Likely causes:**
  1. Stepping in the bucket (scorecard #5) — stride lands toward the dugout.
  2. Pulling off / flying open (scorecard #3).
- **Confirm on video (behind):** Stride foot points toward the dugout, not the pitcher.
- **Fix:** "Stride at the pitcher."
- **Drill:** Stride into a bat laid on the ground as a guide; closed-stride wall drill.

## The "don't fix what isn't broken" rows

Just as important — these results are **good or neutral**. Flagging them as flaws
is how you wreck a hitter who's actually fine.

- **Foul ball straight back:** Usually *on time and on plane*, just barely under
  it. An inch from a line drive. **Leave it alone** — maybe the tiniest "stay
  through it" note, nothing more.
- **Hard line drive / hard contact, any field:** The swing worked. Don't analyze
  it for problems. Note what went *right* so the hitter can repeat it.
- **Swing-and-miss on a genuinely nasty pitch (buried slider, painted corner):**
  That's the pitcher winning, not a swing flaw. Don't manufacture a fix.

---

## Why this structure wins

1. **It dodges subjectivity.** You're not judging style; you're explaining a
   result the hitter already saw. Hard to argue with a rollover grounder.
2. **It agrees with good coaches instead of contradicting them.** Most of these
   causes are timing/initiation — the axis casual coaching skips — so you fill
   the gap rather than fighting over "is the swing good."
3. **It's cheap to automate later.** Each "confirm on video" line is a single
   pose-based check (frame timing, joint angle, stride direction) — and you only
   run the two or three a given result calls for, not all of them on every swing.
